Natural diamond bridal jewellery and its deeper meaning

Given the significance of the occasion, wedding jewellery deserves real consideration. Choose pieces that tell a story, reflect your emotions or capture the spirit of your relationship, regardless of budget.

Formed billions of years ago deep within the Earth’s mantle and brought to the surface through powerful volcanic activity, natural diamonds carry a sense of history that few gemstones can match. Long associated with love, strength and hope, each one is shaped by time and nature, lending it a distinct individuality.

In India, diamonds were discovered as early as 2500 BC. Gemstones from legendary regions like Golconda were worn by emperors and royalty as symbols of power and prestige. Their brilliance and reflective crystal structure led them to be associated with divine light.

The tradition of diamonds in engagement jewellery came much later. In 1477, Archduke Maximilian of Austria is believed to have proposed with a diamond ring, marking one of the earliest recorded engagement rings.
